About Me

I am a PhD student in the hybrid systems and controls lab at the University of New Mexico, and a graduate student researcher at Sandia National Laboratories in the Autonomy for Hypersonics group. My principal research interests include stochastic reachability, theory and applications of kernel methods, statistical learning algorithms, and autonomous systems.

My research is focused on developing algorithms to solve approximate stochastic reachability problems to provide probabilistic guarantees of safety for autonomous and human-in-the-loop systems. I am also interested in applying kernel distribution embeddings to perform estimation and controller synthesis for complex controls problems.
